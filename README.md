# State my Values by Thinking About my Ethical Nightmares
My company/my concentration is cybersecurity, so my ethical nightmare is allowing in mistakes that could have been avoided. Then mistakes end up hurting people's livelihoods, e.g. if the mistake is around money or personal information. So my values would be around taking as many painstaking preventative measures as possible. Then also around responsibility. Specifically that if anything were to go awry, I will have already set up even more rules set in place to stop the security issue from continuing or spreading. Thus, I know my responsibility to the poeple and the great amount of faith they've put in me. 


# Explain Why I Value What I Do in a Way that Connects to my Organization's Mission or Purpose
I am a security professional that keeps safe people's entire livelihoods, everything they work for each day. Even a slight breach can cause someone to loose rent money or money for food for their families. Or worse. Our clients give us control over the part of their lives they can't control on the internet. Thus having infinite security measures in place, and always working as much as I can to stop bad actors, or even accidental security mistakes in code, is my purpose. I will not let even a little breach go because one opening could become a tunnel for bad actors to take. Then thousands of people could be at risk. Thus, prevatatives and responsibility of setting up many measures in place are needed.



# Connect my Values to What I take to be Ethically Impermissible 
Preventatives and responsibility. If I am on a break after a work day and I get a breach alert, I will never just wait until the next day, or let someone less experienced take care of it. I will spend all my time and resources to ensure safety. 


# Articulate How I Will Realize My Ethical Goals Or Avoid My Ethical Nightmares
Authenticity. We will already have procedures for our customers code, to check automatically around the clock for issues. Like with automated functions such as Amazon Guardduty or Amazon AWS. I will pair each company with the checks that both check the current code for if there is any known breaches that could happen, and also if there are any signs of a potential badactor having gotten into the code. If my code allows a bad actor to get in, I will report to the customer, and find as many ways as possible to quickly fix what has happened.
